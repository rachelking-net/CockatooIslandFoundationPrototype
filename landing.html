Transforming Cockatoo Island into a permanent art island

OUR PROPOSAL

The Cockatoo Island Foundation Limited is a not-for-profit charitable foundation campaigning to transform Sydney's historic Cockatoo island into a permanent art island - comparable to Hobart’s Museum of Old and New Art (MONA) in Tasmania and the famous Naoshima art island in Japan. The foundation aims to restore, conserve and maintain the heritage of Cockatoo island, facilitate public appreciation for the island and convert it into a property for the purposes of a permanent museum.

The proposal includes building leases allowed for retail stores, marinas, pop-up stores and other commercial enterprises. The founders, Tony Berg and Danny Goldberg, are prepared to raise between $80 and $100 million in philanthropic funding, whilst also asking the Federal Government to commit a further $200 million to the rehabilitation of the island.
